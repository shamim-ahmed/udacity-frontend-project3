Cat

Cats were venerated in ancient Egypt and it is commonly believed that they were domesticated there.

  • Scientific Name: Felis Catus
  • Average Length: 46 cm
  • Average Weight: 4 to 5 kg
  • Average Lifespan: 12 to 15 years

The domestic cat is a small, typically furry, carnivorous mammal. They are often called house cats when kept as indoor pets or simply cats when there is no need to distinguish them from other felids and felines. Cats are often valued by humans for companionship and for their ability to hunt vermin. There are more than 70 cat breeds.
